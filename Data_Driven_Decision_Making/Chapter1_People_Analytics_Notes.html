People Analytics & The Data Analysis Process

Topic: Applying the six-step process to HR Tags: data-analytics · people-analytics · case-study

Overview

Data is everywhere. Every time you observe and evaluate something, you're collecting and analyzing data. This habit helps you:

  • Find easier ways of doing things
  • Identify patterns that save time
  • Discover new perspectives

This chapter walks through a real-world case study of a team using the six steps of data analysis to solve an HR problem.

What Is People Analytics?

People analytics (also called HR or workforce analytics) is the practice of collecting and analyzing workforce data to improve how a company operates.

A people analyst's goals:

  • Understand employees' work experience
  • Build a more productive, fair workplace
  • Improve retention and engagement

The Six Steps of Data Analysis

#StepPurpose
1AskDefine the problem and success criteria
2PreparePlan timeline, sources, and access rules
3ProcessCollect, clean, and secure data
4AnalyzeFind patterns and insights
5ShareCommunicate findings to stakeholders
6ActImplement changes

Case Study: Reducing New-Hire Turnover

Problem: High turnover — many employees left before completing their first year.

Question: How can the organization improve retention for new employees?

1 Ask

The team worked with leaders to define success. Questions asked:

  • What do new hires need to succeed in year one?
  • Is historical survey data available?
  • Do high-retention managers offer something different?
  • What's the leading cause of dissatisfaction?
  • What retention increase is expected next year?

2 Prepare

A three-month timeline was set. Data would come from an online survey of new employees.

  • Drafted questions on hiring, onboarding, and compensation satisfaction
  • Set access rules: raw data restricted; only aggregated data (e.g., salary ranges) shared externally
  • Finalized what to collect and how to visualize it
  • Identified risks in advance

3 Process

Survey sent out — with consent and transparency as priorities.

  • Restricted data access to a small group of analysts
  • Cleaned data for completeness and accuracy
  • Stored raw data in a secure internal warehouse
Note Ethical handling builds trust for future data collection.

4 Analyze

FindingOutcome
Long, complicated hiring processMore likely to leave
Efficient, transparent feedback processMore likely to stay

All findings were documented, even unflattering ones, to maintain credibility.

5 Share

  • Report shared only with managers meeting a minimum response threshold
  • Results presented to managers first
  • Managers personally delivered results to their own teams

This gave managers context for productive team conversations.

6 Act

Recommendations implemented:

  1. Standardize hiring and evaluation around the most efficient practices
  2. Repeat the survey annually for year-over-year comparison
Result A year later, retention improved — the action plan worked.

Is People Analytics Right for You?

Data analytics stays interesting because every problem is different, solutions take creativity, and the impact on people can be significant.

Action item Note "people analytics" as a field worth exploring further.

Key Takeaways

  • People analytics applies the six-step process to HR challenges
  • Ethics and consent are non-negotiable
  • Transparent hiring and evaluation correlates with retention
  • Sharing through managers drives better action
  • Repeat measurement validates long-term impact
